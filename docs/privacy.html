Privacy Policy

History Quiz: Civilizations & Legends
Last updated: June 2026  •  Effective date: June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Henry Bruskin, sole operator, Vienna, Austria ("we," "us," "our," or the "Operator") collects, uses, and shares personal data when you use the mobile application "History Quiz: Civilizations & Legends" (the "App"). We are the data controller for the personal data described in this policy. If you have questions, contact us at henry@bruskinstudio.com.

1. Summary

We collect a limited amount of data needed to run the game: account and sign-in information, your gameplay progress and leaderboard entries, device and technical information, and analytics and error data. We do not sell your personal data. We use trusted third-party services (such as our backend provider, sign-in providers, analytics, and error reporting) to operate the App.

2. Data we collect

Account and profile data. When you create an account, we collect an identifier for your account and, depending on your sign-in method, your email address and basic profile details supplied by Google or Apple. You can also sign in anonymously, in which case we create an anonymous identifier without an email. You may choose a username, which is shown publicly on the leaderboard.

Gameplay data. We collect data generated as you play, such as your score, XP, coins, lives, eras and questions attempted, accuracy, achievements, and quiz session history. This is used to run the game, save your progress, and populate leaderboards.

Purchase data. When you make a purchase or subscribe, the app store processes your payment. We receive confirmation of your purchase and entitlement status (for example, whether you hold an active subscription), but we do not receive or store your full payment card details.

Device and technical data. We collect technical information such as device type and model, operating system, app version, language settings, and a device or installation identifier. This helps us operate, secure, and troubleshoot the App.

Analytics and diagnostic data. We collect usage events (for example, screens viewed, quizzes completed, features used) and crash and error reports, including technical details about the state of the App when a problem occurred.

Advertising data (planned feature). If and when we introduce advertising, including optional rewarded ads, advertising partners may collect certain device and usage data to serve and measure ads. We will update this policy and, where required, request your consent before such processing begins.

We do not intentionally collect special categories of data (such as health, religion, or precise location), and you should not submit such data through the App.

3. How we use your data and our legal bases

We use personal data to:

  • create and manage your account and provide the game (legal basis: performance of a contract);
  • save your progress and show leaderboards (performance of a contract / our legitimate interest in providing the service);
  • process and verify purchases and subscriptions (performance of a contract);
  • analyze usage to improve and develop the App, and to diagnose and fix errors (our legitimate interest in maintaining and improving the App, or your consent where required);
  • serve and measure advertising, if introduced (your consent where required);
  • keep the App secure and prevent fraud, cheating, and abuse (our legitimate interest, and compliance with legal obligations);
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms.

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. Where we rely on legitimate interests, you may object as described in Section 8.

4. Who we share data with

We share data with service providers who process it on our behalf, and only as needed to run the App:

  • Backend and infrastructure (Supabase) for database, authentication, and hosting to store your account and gameplay data;
  • Sign-in providers (Google, Apple) when you choose to sign in with them;
  • App stores (Apple, Google) for purchase and subscription processing;
  • Subscription management (RevenueCat) to validate entitlements;
  • Analytics providers to understand usage;
  • Error and crash reporting providers to diagnose problems;
  • Advertising partners, if and when advertising is introduced.

We may also disclose data where required by law, to protect our rights or users' safety, or in connection with a business transfer. We do not sell your personal data, and we do not "share" it for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under California law.

5. International transfers

Some of our service providers may process data outside your country, including outside the EEA. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision, as applicable.

6. Data retention

We keep personal data for as long as your account is active and as needed to provide the App. When you delete your account, we delete or anonymize your personal data within a reasonable period, except where we must retain certain data to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements. Aggregated or anonymized data that no longer identifies you may be kept indefinitely.

7. Children's privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child under 13 has provided us personal data, contact us at henry@bruskinstudio.com and we will delete it.

If you are in the EEA, the United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with a minimum digital-consent age, and you are below that age, you may use the App only with the consent of a parent or guardian.

If we introduce advertising, we will not knowingly serve personalized or behaviorally targeted ads to users we know to be children, consistent with applicable law and app store policies.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding your personal data.

EEA / UK (GDPR). You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to processing of your personal data, the right to data portability, and the right to withdraw consent. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (in Austria, the Datenschutzbehörde).

California (CCPA/CPRA). You have the right to know what personal information we collect and how we use and disclose it, the right to delete it, the right to correct it, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising these rights. We do not sell or share personal information as defined by California law.

To exercise any right, contact us at henry@bruskinstudio.com. We will verify your request and respond within the time required by law. You can also delete your account directly in the App.

10. Permissions and choices

Depending on your device, you can control certain data collection through your device settings, including advertising identifiers and notification permissions. You can sign in anonymously to limit the data associated with you, withdraw consent where it applies, and delete your account at any time.
